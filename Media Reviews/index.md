# Astrid Yaps About Media
Do you want to hear my opinion on nooks? Movies? TV shows? That magazine I read when waiting for my friend to shop up at the coffee shop? YouTube videos? Well read away. Everything here is subjective, and I won't be giving number rating because I just want to talk about the art I interact with.

## Books
### 𐂂 [Stag Dance, Torrey Peters](Stag Dance)
Four Stories of Transition, each one just as eqaully gut wrenching as the other.
### ◬ [Kitchen Confidential, Anthony Bourdain](Kitchen Confidential)
A man who has skirted the edge of society, in the guts of a subculture that doesn't exist anymore, tells his story.
## Movies
### ◬ [Smoke(1995)](Smoke)
Once long ago, there was a smoke shop in Brooklyn that one year, became a place were disparate stories interwove.
### ◬ [How To Blow Up A Pipeline(2020)](Pipeline)
A group of climate activists work together to blow up a pipeline whilst causing as little harm as possible.
## TV
### ◬ [Farscape](Farscape)
John Chrichton, astronaut for IASA finds himself flung across the galaxy, and must make his way home with a ragtag group of aliens (feat- puppets by the Jim Hensen Company)
### ◬ [Kill La Kill](Kill_La_Kill)
Fighting fascism and fashion in a standout debut, Studio Trigger talks about the struggle for individuality and bodily autonomy in a beautifully animated show.
## Music
### ◬ [King and Queen of the Losers](KAQOTL)
A small indie folk punk band from Boise Idaho that only released two albums.
## Miscellaneous 
### ◬ [Hazel (Youtube)](Hazel)
If you find joy in listening to your friends talk about the things they love, then Hazel's channel is a wonderful place to go for the digital parasocial equivalent.
