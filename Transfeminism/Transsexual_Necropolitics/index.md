# The Necropolitics of The Transsexual Body; How We Talk About Transsexual existance.

## Introduction and Methodology:
I am currently in the final stages of putting together my preliminary auto-ethnography and argument for why Danish "transition care" constitutes conversion practices. Writing this article has lead me to reflect briefly on the necropolitics of the transexual body. Specifically with regards to our own epistemic authority upon our bodies, as well as how we are marked by society, and the consequences thereof. After these reflection I have decided to pen this essay to briefly put to words my thoughts surrounding how my body, and that of others like me, is a political subject.

Transsexuals are a unique case for necropolitical analysis, partially because our deaths hold an incredible amount of a potential "linguistic violence", a term coined by authors Lindsey B. Stewart and Thomas A. Delgado in their 2023 paper *Necropolitics and Trans Identities: Language Use as Cultural Violence*. However focusing entirely on the political potential of our deaths ignores a large part of the necropolitical position we hold within society, namely that of our disposition as a member of the "living dead" as outlined by philosopher Achille Mbembe in his exploration of necropolitics. It is important to reflect on the ways that the transsexual body is politically captured and marked by it's existence, not just in death but also in life. 

It is precisely because of this need to reflect on the political capturing of the transsexual body as a political device, captured by political discourse, that I find myself writing this essay. I believe that there is an epistemic gap in how the transsexual body is understood and analysed that requires filling. In attempting to fill this epistemic void, the essay will take the form of an omni directional analysis that incorporates a multifaced analysis of how different groups within society view the transsexual body. 

To define the groups that I will be talking about I will be utilizing the gender/sex schema outlined by Julia Serano in her book *Whipping Girl*. I will also be drawing from her work to strengthen my analytical framework on the representations of the transsexual body. I will also be drawing from my paper *Feminism and the Transfeminine* to examine how these categories separate the transsexual further from other categories in the outlined schema. I will then be taking the separate categories outlined by the schema and analysing them in relation to the ways that they interact with transsexual necropolitics. Ultimately I will bring together the separate layers of analysis into a rudimentary outline for how the transsexual body is necropolitically effected.
